*Prologue:*

One knows the depths of solitude, whilst the other, of open-mindedness -yet there is the presence of which is the lack of self.

"To live is to be alive," One said to the other, "It is to be one's self in their own eyes, uncaring of the world's everchanging rhythm."

The other spoke back, "But to live is to be with one another. Our bodies were crafted for warmth and affection, for love and acceptance, and I cannot just throw it all away."

"Oh but I am not telling you to do so."

An eyebrow of the other lad raised. "Then what else is the reason for your preaching of self-worth and idolization of one's self?"

"What I mean to tell you is not for you to throw away your beliefs for a new one but, it is there to exist alongside it." He spoke with a battered tone, "I might have different views but, my intention is to show to you that; purpose can still stand, even in the absence of others."

The man before him pondered before speaking again, "I thought it was either one or the other. Like being either an introvert, or an extrovert."

"It is like having coffee or cake, brother. Why choose one when you can have both?"

"Yes, quite right." He chuckled, "Plus, it makes both the cake and the coffee taste better."

Title : Rule #87
--------------------------------------------------
Series 2 of 7
--------------------------------------------------

It seems like both of Saphira's parents have to attend to business outside of country so they have asked her to go in their stead. A family gathering that the Mortelle couple, her uncle and aunt, set up every single year which everyone from all the family side has to be there, usually the little ones aren't allowed to go but since Saphira is 17 turning 18 soon, her parents assumed it's fine. The Mortelle family has always had strict rules, a nerve-wracking-presence and other things that make a lot from the other family side not want to get in touch with or even attend the family gathering. But for Saphira, she admired them. She only met them twice when she was a little girl, around the age of 3 when her parents had also brought her to the family gathering with which is how they learned about the no-bringing-the-kids-to-the-gathering-rule.

Of course you would be curious as to why they would only know about the rule right there, aren't they supposed to know about it ever since the previous family gathering too? To answer, that one rule was made that very same day too. The day that Saphira was brought for the first time. But Saphira wasn't a naughty kid. She was a very well behaved and intelligent little girl and she still is now.

"There's no need for such rules just because of my daughter",  Saphira's dad said, "she isn't hard to handle and she listens well!"

"Oh no the rule wasn't made because of your daughter, Mr. Remina. My apologies if it comes off that way, think of it as a coincidence alright? Saphira is a smart little girl, we wouldn't create such a rule because of her I assure you", Mrs. Mortelle replied.

"What a terrible liar, maybe hold off planning the yearly family gathering and take some acting class first", Mrs. Remina thought in her head with a glaring expression.

The second time she met them is a hazy memory she can't recall no matter how hard she tries to.

"Hell! I am so late! I am so late!", Saphira panicked as she hurriedly locks her door and hops on a taxi that has waited for her for 5 minutes already, "I am so sorry sir, I'll make sure to pay extra for your time"

"It's alright young lady, use your money to fill your stomach up, I have all day, I mean it's a Sunday after all, and it's only morning too, where do you wish to go?", the old and kind driver responded.

"Oh yes, I would love you to drop me off at this location please", she said as she showed the location on her phone to him.

There was a loud moment of silence and pause, the atmosphere feels completely different from 20 seconds ago when they started their conversation. Saphira couldn't see the driver's facial expression as her phone that she was holding up to show him the location was blocking her view but when she had put her phone back down, the driver had already turned to the front and then said, "I'll get you to that place in 30 minutes, are you ready to go?" His voice sounds completely normal leaving Saphira to wonder what caused the sudden change in atmosphere just now. Or was it all just her imagination?
